About

My name is Billy Mejia, and I am a dedicated and passionate educator committed to fostering student growth and academic excellence. I hold a Bachelor of Arts in History with a minor in Education from the University of California, Merced, where I also earned my Preliminary Single Subject Teaching Credential in Social Science.

I have been interested in education and the transformative power of teaching since high school. My instructional approach focuses on fostering critical thinking, civic engagement, and meaningful connections to real-world issues. I strive to create a supportive, inclusive, and intellectually stimulating learning environment for all students.

My experience includes designing standards-aligned lessons, facilitating simulations and seminars, integrating technology in the classroom, and adapting instruction to meet diverse learning needs. I am particularly passionate about curriculum design that encourages student voice, inquiry, and active participation.

To learn more about my professional background, credentials, and teaching philosophy, please refer to my resume or reach out directly.

My goal is to foster a learning environment where students feel supported, challenged, and inspired to reach their full potential.

Beyond academic achievement, I strive to create a classroom experience that students will remember positively. School should be a place where students not only gain knowledge, but also form lasting memories, build confidence, and discover who they are.

As a history teacher, I am especially passionate about helping students view the past with fresh eyes. I want to make history engaging, relevant, and reflective of diverse perspectives. Ultimately, I hope to inspire an appreciation for the work of historians and the importance of learning from our shared past.
