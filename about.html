About

Billy Mejia is a dedicated and passionate educator with a strong commitment to student growth and academic excellence. He holds a Bachelor of Arts in History with a minor in Education from the University of California, Merced, where he also earned his Preliminary Single Subject Teaching Credential in Social Science.

Since high school, Billy has been deeply interested in education and the transformative impact of teaching. His instructional approach focuses on fostering critical thinking, civic engagement, and meaningful connections to real-world issues. He strives to create a supportive, inclusive, and intellectually stimulating learning environment for all students.

Billy’s experience includes designing standards-aligned lessons, facilitating simulations and seminars, integrating technology in the classroom, and adapting instruction to meet diverse learning needs. He is especially passionate about curriculum design that encourages student voice, inquiry, and active participation.

To learn more about his professional background, credentials, and teaching philosophy, please refer to his resume or reach out directly.

Philosophy of Teaching

As an educator, my philosophy of teaching centers on providing every student with an equitable opportunity to succeed. I believe that education should be inclusive, accessible, and empowering for all learners—regardless of their background or circumstances. My goal is to foster a learning environment where students feel supported, challenged, and inspired to reach their full potential.

Beyond academic achievement, I strive to create a classroom experience that students will remember positively. I aim to build meaningful relationships, promote curiosity, and cultivate a sense of belonging. School should be a place where students not only gain knowledge, but also form lasting memories, build confidence, and discover who they are.

As a history teacher, I am especially passionate about helping students view the past with fresh eyes. I want to make history engaging, relevant, and reflective of diverse perspectives. I believe in the power of storytelling and historical thinking to help students understand the world around them and their role within it. Ultimately, I hope to inspire an appreciation for the work of historians and the importance of learning from our shared past.
